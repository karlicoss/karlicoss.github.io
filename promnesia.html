Promnesia is a browser extension (Chrome/Firefox/Firefox mobile) which serves as a web surfing copilot, enhancing your browsing history, improving the web exploration experience and integrating it with your knowledge base.

The repository contains some demos and the setup guide, this post is more of a motivation and the story of what has led me to work on it.

I've been raised on science fiction, and growing up, I've been dreaming about the technology drastically changing our lives. Augmenting the brain with a math coprocessor, neural interfaces, having perfect reaction and memory – many of you are on the same page and know the drill.

Years had passed, I became a software engineer and had realised, how far are we from all these fancy technologies I wanted. So my dreams? (TODO) have become more modest, and I've chosen a more realistic and plausible target: using my digital trace like browser history to compensate for my limited memory.

I'm exploring lots of information on the Internet, and it feels wasteful to use my meat resources (TODO tag) to keep track of it. Web browsers tracks it automatically, without any manual and conscious effort.

Surely we can benefit from the immense computing and memory capabilities of the computer?

So I figured that I wanted:

¶1 I want a browsing assistant

A copilot. A web surfing companion. A Remembrance Agent. Call it whatever you like, I wish it was a concept with a widely accepted name.

Something that would:

  • aid me with information processing

    Keep track of what I've read, on what device, and for how long.

  • show me my annotations and highlights, within the original page

    I don't want to keep wondering whether I got my throughts stashed in some app. (TODO)

  • help me to explore new information and prioritize it

    There is lots of awesome stuff on the Internet and the biggest problem I have is picking the most relevant things to dig in.

    I wish there was something that could in a single click show me, who of my friends, or of the people I follow, have read a certain link. What did they think? Have they posted about it, annotated or something else?

  • link/connect together TODO

  • unify my scattered and siloed bookmarks

    Browser bookmarks; Reddit/Hackernews saves; "Watch later" on Youtube, saved links in your instant messenger.

    These are the same damn thing, yet there is no way to oversee and process them through a single interface.

  • bridge the gap between the browser and my private wiki

I wanted a system like this for several years.

At some point, after failed attempts at finding an existing solution (TODO link), I'd figured that it was going to be a yet another thing I had to implement myself.

The journey to build this took longer than I expected, led me theought a rabbithole and lots of yak shaving, and made me realize: browser history is very, very broken.

¶2 Browser history is broken

Browsing history is a rich source of potentially useful information. Just think about it: it's lifelogging for free. It contains exact timestamps and links which basically address information.

To some extent, URLs express relationships like persons/groups/TODO?? we have for free. Seems like a rich/ripe TODO for TODO, right? You should be able to know TODO, associate TODO, and be able to easily trace your jumps through the websites.

Now think what experience do you have instead. What was the last time you used it in any nontrivial way, for something other than reopening an accidentally closed tab?

In addition:

¶it's not just about web browsers

If you're using a native phone app (say, for Reddit or Twitter), you history either isn't logged anywhere or stored locally on your phone.

On Android, it's likely to be in an Sqlite database in /data/data/app.name. This directory is not accessible to normal users, unless your phone is rooted. Just think about how ridiculous it is. It's your own data, yet your OS babysits you and prevents your from accessing it.
rooting modern Android phones ranges from "somewhat painful" to "very painful"

As a specific example, Reddit Slide app keeps the view history in /data/data/me.ccrama.redditslide/SEEN. Even if you root your phone and can access the database, because the model TODO data is flawed?? in the first place, the app isn't persisting the data in the database, it's merely a cache TODO latest few weeks or something? So if you want your complete history, you have to backup this database regularly, keep the snapshots and somehow reconstruct it.

You may dismiss this as data nitpicking, but when all of your phone apps are doing this, you're missing out on quite a lot of information.

¶it's got different weight (TODO not sure about 'weight'?)

Not all links in your history are equal (on equal/the same grounds??).

  • some are clicked on by accident
  • some you've just skimmed
  • some are on your reading list
  • some you've been reading for hours and they are full of your highlights and annotations
  • some you are keeping in your knowledge base/personal wiki
  • some of them you've shared with other people on social media

The current browser history experience makes absolutely no difference between these scenarios.

There is a family of Chrome extensions similar to History Trends Unlimited, which try to make up for Chrome 90 day retention by keeping the history in IndexDB.

The only problem they solve is retention, and it ends up as a yet another silo.

(but I guess 30K users at least shows there is a demand for such functionality)
